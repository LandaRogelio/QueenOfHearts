General Information on Cardiovascular Disease (CVD)


Heart Disease is the #1 killer of women in the U.S., killing more women than all cancers combined. It claims nearly 400,000 women's lives each year; That's one death every 90 seconds. Nearly half of all women (44%) were not aware that CVD is their leading cause of death. Among Black and Hispanic women, 63% and 66%, respectively, were not aware.


Signs and Symptoms of CVD

Knowledge is power. Look below to see if you have any symptoms corresponding to CVD:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Pain in your arm(s), back, or neck
  • Stomach pain or severe abdominal pressure
  • Shortness of breath, nausea or lightheadedness
  • Sweating
  • Fatigue

Factors That Increase Your Risk for Heart Disease

Below are factors that increase your chance of getting heart disease:
  • Lack of physical activity
  • High LDL cholesterol
  • High Blood Pressure
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity
